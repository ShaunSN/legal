Relevant Constitutional Provisions

Section 32 - Right to Freedom

Constitution of Papua New Guinea

(1) Freedom based on law consists in the least amount of restriction on the activities of individuals that is consistent with the maintenance and development of Papua New Guinea and of society in accordance with this Constitution and, in particular, with the National Goals and Directive Principles and the Basic Social Obligations.

(2) Every person has the right to freedom based on law, and accordingly has a legal right to do anything that–

  • (a) does not injure or interfere with the rights and freedoms of others; and
  • (b) is not prohibited by law,

and no person–

  • (c) is obliged to do anything that is not required by law; and
  • (d) may be prevented from doing anything that complies with the provisions of paragraphs (a) and (b).

Application to Your Case:

You cannot be obliged to pay bride price as it is not required by law. Your freedom to reject customs conflicting with your beliefs is protected.

Section 41 - Proscribed Acts

(1) Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in any other provision of any law, any act that is done under a valid law but in the particular case–

  • (a) is harsh or oppressive; or
  • (b) is not warranted by, or is disproportionate to, the requirements of the particular circumstances or of the particular case; or
  • (c) is otherwise not, in the particular circumstances, reasonably justifiable in a democratic society having a proper regard for the rights and dignity of mankind,

is an unlawful act.

Application to Your Case:

Threats to separate your family unless you pay bride price are harsh, oppressive, and not reasonably justifiable in a democratic society.

Section 55 - Equality of Citizens

(1) Subject to this Constitution, all citizens have the same rights, privileges, obligations and duties irrespective of race, tribe, place of origin, political opinion, colour, creed, religion or sex.

Application to Your Case:

You cannot be discriminated against or subjected to different obligations based on tribal customs that you do not practice.

Section 57 - Enforcement of Guaranteed Rights and Freedoms

(1) A right or freedom referred to in this Division shall be protected by, and is enforceable in, the Supreme Court or the National Court or any other court prescribed for the purpose by an Act of the Parliament, either on its own initiative or on application by any person who has an interest in its protection and enforcement...

(3) A court that has jurisdiction under Subsection (1) may make all such orders and declarations as are necessary or appropriate for the purposes of this section...

(5) Relief under this section is not limited to cases of actual or imminent infringement of the guaranteed rights and freedoms, but may, if the court thinks it proper to do so, be given in cases in which there is a reasonable probability of infringement, or in which an action that a person reasonably desires to take is inhibited by the likelihood of, or a reasonable fear of, an infringement.

Your Enforcement Rights:

You can apply to the National Court for protection even before actual harm occurs - the reasonable fear of family separation is sufficient grounds for relief.

Key Legal Precedent

In re Miriam Willingal [1997] PNGLR 119

Case Facts:

  • • Young woman demanded as part of customary "head pay" compensation
  • • Threatened with forced customary marriage
  • • Constitutional enforcement application filed under Section 57

Court Holdings:

  • • Custom violated Sections 32, 36, 42, 49, 52, and 55 of Constitution
  • • Custom was inconsistent with Customs Recognition Act
  • • Custom was repugnant to general principles of humanity

Relevance to Your Case:

This case establishes that customary practices cannot override constitutional rights and that courts will issue protective orders against coercive customs.

Family Protection Act 2013

Additional Protection Available

The Family Protection Act 2013 provides additional remedies for domestic violence, which includes psychological abuse and threats:

Section 5 - Domestic Violence Includes:

  • • Psychological abuse, harassment or intimidation
  • • Threats to do harmful acts
  • • Stalking causing apprehension or fear

Available Orders:

  • • Interim Protection Orders (immediate relief)
  • • Full Protection Orders (up to 2 years)
  • • No-contact and no-approach conditions

Important Provision - Section 6(2)

"It is not a defence to an offence under Subsection (1) that the defendant has paid an amount of money or given other valuable consideration, in accordance with his or her custom, to the complainant."

This means bride price payments cannot justify domestic violence or threats.
